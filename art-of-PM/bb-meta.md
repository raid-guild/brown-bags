## Abstract

ECWireless breaks down the keys to running a successful RaidGuild project through three pillars: scope, team, and pace. The session focuses on defining realistic project boundaries, assembling and trusting the right contributors, setting role clarity, managing blockers, and keeping delivery momentum without losing client context.

## Transcript Summary

ECWireless frames project management in RaidGuild as context ownership. The monk or PM needs to understand the client's goals, translate them into a defensible scope, and keep the whole team confident that decisions are grounded in reality. The talk repeatedly returns to the idea that projects expand by default, so the PM's job is to define thick boundaries, set expectations early, and communicate clearly when blockers or estimation errors appear.

The second half focuses on team dynamics: choose contributors carefully, define role boundaries, trust people to work in their strengths, and remove bad fits early. Pace is treated as a leadership function, not just a schedule: regular meetings, quiet space for blockers to surface, and visible progress all help keep a raid healthy.
