## Abstract

Elco presents Bard School, a practical content system for DAOs and communities. The talk frames content as coordination infrastructure, using buckets, long-form seeds, awareness funnels, lightweight rituals, and a minimal publishing cadence to help RaidGuild produce consistent, useful public artifacts.

## Transcript Summary

Elco argues that RaidGuild has a "headless brand" problem: everyone carries a different version of the guild in their head, yet the organization still needs regular public content that is useful, coherent, and credible. Bard School proposes a minimum viable content system built around collecting raw material in buckets, shaping substantial "seed" artifacts, and deriving lighter social posts or videos from those seeds.

The talk treats content as operational infrastructure rather than marketing decoration. A small cadence, clear awareness funnel, and repeatable rituals are presented as a way to avoid bursts of brand activity that fade out. AI is positioned as a production assistant, but human taste and evaluation remain central.
