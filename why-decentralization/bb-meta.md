## Abstract

Sky explains why decentralization matters beyond slogans, grounding the discussion in resilience, agency, governance, trust, and the risks of centralized control. The session explores decentralization as both a technical and social design choice for communities, protocols, and institutions.

## Transcript Summary

Sky frames decentralization as a spectrum and a response to concrete problems with centralized systems: money printing, access control, censorship, product shutdowns, and brittle institutional power. The session is intentionally conversational, inviting interruptions and group discussion rather than treating decentralization as a fixed doctrine.

The talk also draws on DAO experience, including the fact that DAOs can dissolve, stagnate, or complete their purpose. Decentralization matters because it changes who can act, who can stop others from acting, and how communities can form or end without relying on a single central authority.
