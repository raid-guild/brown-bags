## Abstract

0xRowdy presents The Juice in the context of product development. The session discusses how to shape product ideas, evaluate what is useful, and move from concept toward practical execution while keeping users, workflows, and delivery constraints in view.

## Transcript Summary

0xRowdy frames "the juice" as the value hidden inside problems. The session argues that good product ideas often start as internal pain points: if a community or team is struggling with something, others probably are too. The builder's job is to notice those problems, solve them locally, and then recognize when the solution could become an external product.

The talk uses examples from the RaidGuild, DAOhaus, and broader web3 tooling ecosystem to show how repeated coordination problems become software opportunities. It encourages new builders to ask questions, pay attention to friction, and treat their own workflow problems as product discovery.
