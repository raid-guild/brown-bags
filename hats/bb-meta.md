## Abstract

Spencer introduces Hats and its approach to roles, permissions, and organizational structure. The session explores how programmable role systems can support DAO operations, clarify responsibilities, and encode access or authority in ways that are composable with onchain coordination.

## Transcript Summary

Spencer uses the session to place Hats inside a longer journey through DAOs and organizational tooling. The talk starts from why DAOs are compelling, then moves toward the practical problem of roles: who can do what, how responsibility is assigned, and how communities can make authority legible without defaulting to vague social process.

Hats is presented as infrastructure for encoding roles, permissions, and revocation in a more composable way. The session connects role design to onboarding, contribution paths, governance, and the reality that healthy organizations need both human trust and explicit operating structure.
