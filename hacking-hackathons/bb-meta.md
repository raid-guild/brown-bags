## Abstract

This session looks at hackathons as a practical environment for shipping, learning, and creating opportunities. It covers how to approach hackathon work strategically, scope ideas down, collaborate under time pressure, and turn quick experiments into demos, portfolio pieces, or follow-on projects.

## Transcript Summary

The speaker explains hackathons as a leverage point for career growth, not just short competitions. Early wins led to money, interviews, roadmap influence, and paid work, but the main argument is that hackathons create concentrated opportunities to learn, ship, and become visible to organizations that need builders.

The tactical advice is to spend serious time on the idea, understand what judges and sponsors actually want, and build something that serves the sponsor's ecosystem. The session emphasizes scoping, narrative, solo execution, and using bounties or hackathons as a way to open doors that would otherwise be hard to access.
