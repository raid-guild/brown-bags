## Abstract

TheKerp makes the case for why RaidGuild matters as a model for the future of work. The session reflects on decentralized collaboration, guild-based opportunity, autonomy, reputation, and how RaidGuild's structure creates a different path from conventional employment.

## Transcript Summary

TheKerp uses RaidGuild as a concrete example of what the future of work can look like: flexible, reputation-based, collaborative, and built around networks instead of corporate hierarchy. The talk blends personal context with a broader argument that guilds and DAOs let people coordinate around shared opportunity without needing conventional employment structures.

The session also points to the emotional and social dimensions of work: why people get excited about RaidGuild, how communities create momentum, and how autonomy changes the relationship between labor, learning, and identity. RaidGuild is presented less as a perfect institution and more as a living experiment in self-directed collaboration.
