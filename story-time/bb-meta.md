## Abstract

Christina and BorrowLucid lead a story-focused session about content, attention, and narrative practice. The brown bag includes a writing exercise and discussion about how to tell stories from perspective, use sensory detail, and think about audience without polluting the internet with low-signal content.

## Transcript Summary

The session begins with a compact content framework: know who you are trying to reach, where they spend attention, what they want, and what you want them to do. From there it becomes a writing exercise focused on perspective and sensory detail.

Participants are asked to write the story of waking up from first-person perspective without relying on "I," "me," or "my." The exercise is designed to shift attention away from explanation and toward perception, showing how story can communicate feeling through what the narrator notices.
