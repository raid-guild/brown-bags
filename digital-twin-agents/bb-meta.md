## Abstract

James Young explores Digital Twin Agents as AI representatives for DAO members and decentralized organizations. The session covers preference learning, coherence scores, recursive trust networks, delegated decision-making, multi-agent governance, and how alignment-focused agents could expand coordination beyond token-weighted voting.

## Transcript Summary

James Young introduces Digital Twin Agents as a way to scale trust and alignment in decentralized organizations. The core idea is that agents can learn a person's preferences through repeated prediction and feedback, then serve as a representative in organizational contexts where humans cannot participate in every decision.

The conversation moves from individual agents to networks of agents that can reason about trust recursively. Rather than relying only on token voting, the model imagines coherence scores, delegated decision-making, and agent-mediated governance. The session also surfaces open questions around alignment, safety, and how much authority people should give to AI representatives.
