## Abstract

This session covers how to build a sustainable web3 career through local meetups, small bounties, open source work, client relationships, and community reputation. It emphasizes business development as a practical skill for independent builders: finding early opportunities, positioning around market narratives, and using RaidGuild as a network for compounding work.

## Transcript Summary

The speaker gives a practical, personal map for breaking into web3 work: claim small opportunities, build a visible network, take bounties, show up at local meetups, and use every project as a reputation signal. The session is especially direct about the first year or two being difficult, with low pay, side income, and lots of learning through imperfect work.

The larger lesson is that independent work requires more than engineering skill. Builders need to understand sales, narratives, client communication, trend positioning, and community participation. RaidGuild is presented as a place where opportunity compounds for people who show up, volunteer for real work, and convert small openings into larger client relationships.
